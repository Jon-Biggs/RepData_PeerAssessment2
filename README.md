# An Analysis of the Population Health and Economic Impact by Severe Weather Events - Based on U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's storm database

# Synopsis

This report is an analysis of the impact of different weather events on public health and economy based on the storm database collected from the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administrationâ€™s (NOAA). The data used were estimates of fatalities, injuries, property and crop damage (since 1996) to determine which types of event are most harmful to the population health and economy. It was found that high temperatures and tornado were most harmful with respect to population health, while tornadoes/high wind had the greatest economic impacts.
